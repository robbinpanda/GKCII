Abstract: In this paper, reinforcement learning is used to solve the reconnaissance and strike tasks of UGVs and UAVs in the
battlefield environment, and different types of agents are trained separately under the premise of environmental interaction. As a
mature reinforcement learning algorithm, SAC algorithm has been widely used in the training of single agents. By assigning part
of the state of the UGV to the UAV, the problem that the fully distributed structure does not converge is solved. For the actual
battlefield environment, UGVs and UAVs need to undertake different tasks. The UAV provides accompanying vigilance for
UGV and the main reconnaissance task of the target area. The UGV should go to different target areas according to task priority
order to carry out auxiliary reconnaissance and strike tasks after reconnaissance.
Key Words: Reinforcement learning, Soft Actor-Critic, UGV and UAV system

Introduction

In recent years, the rapid advancement of unmanned
systems has significantly reshaped modern warfare and
complex mission planning. Among these systems,
Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) and Unmanned Ground
Vehicles (UGVs) have garnered considerable attention due
to their complementary capabilities. UAVs, with their
superior mobility and wide-area surveillance capabilities,
excel in reconnaissance, target detection, and real-time
situational awareness. UGVs, on the other hand, possess
advantages in persistent ground operations, precision
engagement, and logistics, making them indispensable in a
variety of mission scenarios. By combining these strengths,
UAV-UGV collaborative systems offer a powerful solution
for achieving efficient, coordinated, and intelligent strike
operations.
The integration of UAVs and UGVs for cooperative
missions poses several critical challenges ([1]-[3]). These
include the design of robust coordination strategies ([4]),
seamless communication protocols, and adaptive
decision-making frameworks to address dynamic and
uncertain environments. Furthermore, ensuring efficient
resource allocation, minimizing operational latency, and
maximizing mission success in high-stakes scenarios are key
areas of focus. In the context of collaborative strike missions,
the ability of UAVs to scout and identify targets in real-time,
coupled with the precision of UGVs to engage these targets
effectively, exemplifies the potential of such systems to
outperform traditional unmanned operations ([5]). Recent
advancements in artificial intelligence (AI), particularly in
reinforcement learning, have provided promising avenues for
addressing these challenges.
Reinforcement Learning (RL) has emerged as a powerful
paradigm for enabling intelligent agents to learn optimal
behaviors through interaction with their environments. By
maximizing cumulative rewards, RL algorithms allow agents
to autonomously discover strategies that balance exploration
and exploitation.[6] Among the most notable advancements
are model-free RL methods, such as Q-learning([7]) and

979-8-3503-5731-8/25/$31.00 ┬ę2025 IEEE

Policy Gradient approaches, which have demonstrated
success in high-dimensional and continuous action spaces.
More recently, algorithms like Deep Deterministic Policy
Gradient (DDPG)([8]-[10]) and Soft Actor-Critic (SAC)
([11],[12]),have leveraged deep neural networks to extend
RL to complex tasks involving continuous control, offering
greater stability and sample efficiency.
This paper investigates a novel framework for UAV-UGV
collaborative strike missions, focusing on optimizing their
coordination through advanced reinforcement learning
techniques. Specifically, we design a system where UAVs
perform real-time reconnaissance to identify and classify
targets, while UGVs execute precise strike actions informed
by the UAV-provided intelligence. Experimental results
show that the framework can select the maneuvering path
and attack point automatically, and realize the good
cooperation ability of UAV and UGV.

SAC introduces the concept of maximum entropy whose
goal is to learn a policy that maximizes a weighted sum of

The SAC algorithm has become an important tool in the
field of reinforcement learning due to its characteristic of
maximizing entropy, its ability to adapt to continuous and
high-dimensional spaces, as well as its strong sample
efficiency and stability. Especially when it comes to
balancing exploration and exploitation, solving complex
tasks and high-dimensional problems, SAC demonstrates
superior performance. The method of SAC not only plays a
role in single-agent reinforcement learning but also performs
well in multi-agent reinforcement learning in the algorithm
MASAC ([13]). And if the strategy of centralized training
and Decentralized Execution (CTDE) is used, the
information is centralized and the strategy of the agents is
jointly updated during training, and the agents make
decisions independently based on their local information and
strategy during execution. CTDE is a commonly used
framework in multi-agent systems, where at training time all
agents share information about the environment and train
jointly, but each agent makes decisions based on its own
observations at execution time. In this way, despite their
different state and action space dimensions, the overall
system behavior can be optimized by designing an
appropriate joint learning strategy. For the system of single
UGV and single UAV, the joint state dimension of
unmanned vehicle and UAV will bring large computational
overhead.
If we consider updating the state in the environment at the
same time, but instead of using centralized training, we
generate their own experience pools, sample and train them
separately, and then execute them separately. In the system
of UGV and UAV, the high mobility of UAV can help UGV
to complete the reconnaissance of the target area, and the
enemy units in the target area can be detected earlier, so that
the UGV can strike in a short time. At the same time, on the
way, the UAV can learn the information of the UGV, which
can provide a charging platform for the UAV.
