Integrated Task Scheduling, Path Planning, and
Control for Cooperative UGV–UAV Systems via an
Extended MPPI-GA Framework
DA-HYUN NAM

AND HYEONG-GEUN KIM

Department of Mechanical Engineering, Incheon National University, Incheon 22012, Republic of Korea

Corresponding author: Hyeong-Geun Kim (hgkim@inu.ac.kr)
This work was supported by Korean Government Korea AeroSpace Administration (KASA) under Grant RS-2022-NR067080.

ABSTRACT This study presents a cooperative algorithm for heterogeneous unmanned ground and
aerial vehicles (UGVs and UAVs). Such cooperative systems have recently garnered significant attention
in domains like disaster response, target tracking, and exploration. However, many existing methods
are constrained by hierarchical structures for task assignment, path planning, and control, which limits
adaptability in dynamic environments. Furthermore, these approaches often rely on clustering processes,
with performance frequently sensitive to the chosen granularity. To address these limitations, this work
extends the previously proposed model predictive path integral control-genetic algorithm (MPPI-GA)
framework, initially developed for UGVs, to quadrotor UAVs, resulting in an output-sampled MPPI-GA
(oMPPI-GA), by adapting it to their dynamic characteristics. Building upon this extension, a unified
algorithm is proposed that integrates task scheduling, obstacle-avoidance path planning, and control input
generation into a single, cohesive framework. The algorithm adopts an output-sampled MPPI structure to
alleviate control input sampling sensitivity for UAVs, and a novel triple-layer chromosome enabling UAVs
to generate closed tours without explicit task clustering. In this system, UAVs equipped with oMPPI-GA
and UGVs using MPPI-GA cooperatively execute missions. A distributed task conflict resolution scheme
facilitates seamless task allocation among these heterogeneous agents. The effectiveness of the proposed
algorithm is validated through numerical simulations and real-world hardware experiments.

Other studies have explored UGV-dominant frameworks [8], [9], in which the UGV serves as the primary

In non-dominant cooperative systems, both UGVs and
UAVs actively execute their respective tasks while complementing each other’s capabilities, with UAVs providing
wide-area and terrain-adaptive coverage and rapid mobility,
and UGVs offering endurance and ground-level support. Mission planning under communication constraints is addressed
in [10], where an MILP formulation is solved using
a branch-and-cut algorithm to obtain optimal solutions.
A hybrid genetic algorithm is introduced in [11] to allocate
tasks between UGVs and UAVs in a distributed manner.
Reference [12] assigns exploration responsibilities to both
platforms while also utilizing the UGV as a mobile charging
asset; the authors combine classical combinatorial techniques
with evolutionary methods to construct their strategy. Finally,
[13] proposes a quantum-classical hybrid algorithm that
merges quantum evolutionary search with traditional genetic
algorithms, addressing mission allocation across heterogeneous agents including UAVs, UGVs, and unmanned surface
vehicles (USVs).
While some approaches have explored distributed task
allocation, many of the aforementioned studies predominantly rely on centralized or hierarchical architectures for
overall mission planning and control [14]. In practice,
however, such hierarchical frameworks can limit real-time
adaptability and mission execution efficiency due to sequential processing and rigid task structures. Furthermore, many
existing algorithms assign tasks assuming a 2D environment,
even though UAVs operate in three-dimensional space during
mission execution.
Recently, decentralized cooperative algorithms have also
been developed. For instance, [15] proposes a graph neural
operator-based approach that utilizes a graph attention task
allocator for optimized target detection. However, in this

In this study, we aim to enable both UGVs and UAVs
to perform their respective tasks effectively: the UGV
undertakes ground-level tasks, and the UAV handles aerial
tasks, with all operations considered in a three-dimensional
space. Rather than relying on a hierarchical architecture,
we adopt an integrated algorithm that simultaneously performs task scheduling, obstacle-avoidance path planning,
and plant-level control to enhance real-time responsiveness.
To this end, we build upon our previously developed
MPPI–GA algorithm [17], an integrated framework capable
of handling these aspects within a single algorithmic loop.
Compared with hierarchical architectures, MPPI-GA is
better suited to adapting to dynamic mission scenarios
and efficiently generating obstacle-avoiding trajectories.
Furthermore, the generalized MPPI-GA naturally extends
to multi-agent systems in a decentralized manner, ensuring
scalability while preserving real-time responsiveness.
However, the original MPPI-GA was limited to planar
UGV operations and therefore could not be directly applied
to aerial platforms operating in three-dimensional space.
In this work, we extend and adapt MPPI-GA for quadrotor
UAVs, resulting in an output-sampled MPPI-GA (oMPPIGA), by tailoring it to their specific dynamic characteristics.
Building upon this extension, we further propose a cooperative algorithm that facilitates seamless collaboration between
UAVs and UGVs in heterogeneous multi-agent systems.
In this cooperative algorithm, the UGV follows an open tour
to complete its assigned ground tasks, with each ground task
location serving as a candidate takeoff and landing point for
the UAV. From these candidate locations, the UAV performs

Although quadrotor UAVs appear to move omnidirectionally, their translational accelerations are inherently
coupled with roll–pitch attitude changes and bounded thrust,
resulting in nonholonomic-like dynamic constraints. Such
characteristics must be considered even in large-scale 3D
environments, where long-distance flight accumulates the
effects of limited acceleration, attitude transitions, and
disturbances. Therefore, incorporating UAV dynamics is
essential to ensure that the planned aerial trajectories remain
feasible and reliable for real execution.
To summarize, the main contributions of this paper are as
follows: 1. We propose an extended version of MPPI-GA
applicable to quadrotor UAVs (oMPPI-GA). 2. We establish
a cooperative planning framework combining oMPPI-GA
for UAVs and MPPI-GA for UGVs in a decentralized
setting. 3. The proposed method explicitly considers mission
execution in three-dimensional environments. 4. The UAV
can generate and execute its aerial task sequence without
requiring a separate clustering algorithm, facilitated by the
proposed triple-layer chromosome structure. 5. We propose
a three-dimensional output sampling region for oMPPI,
enabling its robust application to quadrotor UAVs.

II. PRELIMINARIES

Before presenting our algorithms, this section briefly reviews
essential background concepts. Subsection II-A provides
an overview of the MPPI-GA algorithm. Subsections II-B
and II-C then describe the dynamic models of the UGV and
UAV, respectively, employed in this study.
A. MPPI-GA

The TSP is widely recognized as an NP-hard problem [18],
[19]. Consequently, heuristic approaches such as genetic
algorithm (GA) have been extensively employed to find
near-optimal solutions [20], [21], [22]. We have recently
proposed MPPI-GA, which integrates model predictive
path integral control (MPPI) with a GA by leveraging
their structural similarities [17]. MPPI-GA synergistically
combines the sampling-based optimization capability of
MPPI with the evolutionary search mechanism of a GA.
MPPI is particularly well-suited for handling nonconvex cost
functions and nonlinear system dynamics [23], [24], while
GA enables efficient exploration in discrete task allocation
scenarios. This unified algorithm performs task scheduling,
obstacle-avoidance path planning, and control input genera2310

tion, all within a single iterative loop. For further algorithmic
details and its formal formulation, readers are referred to our
previous work.
