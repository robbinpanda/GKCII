Abstract— This paper presents the development of a modelbased design approach for a tethered unmanned aerial vehicle
(UAV) and unmanned ground vehicle (UGV) system. An
integrated dynamic model is formulated to capture the coupled
dynamics of the UAV, tether, and winch mechanism. A Sugenotype fuzzy inference system (FIS) is employed to adaptively
adjust the reference tether length based on wind velocity and the
desired geometric separation between the UAV and UGV. A
dual-loop PID/PI control strategy with switching logic is
implemented to regulate both tether length and winch motor
velocity, enabling robust tracking of the reference tether length.
Simulation results demonstrate the effectiveness of the
integrated.

B. Novelty of the Research
While the literature addresses elements of UAV attitude
control and tether management, there is little work on the
development of an integrated, model-based design that
explicitly captures the interactions between the UAV, tether,
and winch subsystems and couples this with an adaptive
tether-length policy. This paper contributes:
• A unified dynamic model combining UAV rigidbody motion, tether behavior, and winch
electromechanics.
• An adaptive tether-length strategy using a Sugenotype fuzzy inference system (FIS) to maintain
positive tension under wind.
• A coordinated dual-loop control architecture in
which UAV stabilisation and winch operation are
jointly managed.

INTRODUCTION

Tethered Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) have gained
significant attention in recent years. Compared to free-flying
UAVs, tethered systems overcome the limitations of onboard
battery capacity and provide secure, interference-resistant
communication links. These characteristics position tethered
UAVs as a viable alternative for missions requiring extended
operation and high communication reliability.
A. Literature Review
Existing research highlights the suitability of tethered
UAV platforms for long-duration missions such as
surveillance, emergency response, and telecommunications,
see [1], [2], [3] and [4]. The presence of a tether, however,
significantly complicates the system dynamics. Its nonlinear
and coupled behaviour, driven by catenary geometry and
aerodynamic drag, introduces a dynamic constraint that
strongly influences UAV motion, particularly under wind
disturbances [5]. Studies by Nicotra et al. [5] and Yan et al.
[6] model both taut and slack cable regimes, showing that
cable mass and length variation induce oscillatory effects and
additional control challenges. Marques et al. [3] further
emphasised that effective tether management must address
both mechanical stability and control adaptability in the
presence of environmental perturbations.
Classical PID control has been the predominant approach
for regulating UAV attitude and tether behaviour due to its
simplicity and ease of implementation [7]. Yet fixed-gain
PID controllers struggle with the nonlinearities and parameter
variations introduced by changing tether forces and wind
gusts. To overcome these limitations, Musa [8] reviewed
quadcopter control strategies and showed that fuzzy logic and
neural networks offer improved adaptability to real-time
disturbances. Misin [9] demonstrated that Model Predictive
Control (MPC) can maintain UAV stability by predicting
future states and optimising control actions under constraints.

supply to the UAV and enables bidirectional data
communication with the UGV. A motor-driven winch
mounted on the UGV adjusts the tether length dynamically
during operation via an actuated spool mechanism.
