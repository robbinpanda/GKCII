Robust Distributed Formation Control for
Heterogeneous UAVs–UGVs Systems: An
Adaptive Asymptotic Funnel
Control Approach
Yancheng Yan , Hongjing Liang , Member, IEEE, Yuhua Cheng , Senior Member, IEEE,
and Tieshan Li , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—This paper is concerned with the distributed formation control problem of heterogeneous uncrewed aerial vehiclesuncrewed ground vehicles (UAVs-UGVs) systems, in the presence
of practical factors such as parametric and dynamics uncertainties, external disturbances, coupled dynamics, and communication noises. First, we establish a model for the heterogeneous
UAVs-UGVs system with such practical factors. Subsequently, a
new robust distributed formation control strategy is developed.
To be specific, by implementing a delicate transformation with a
special funnel function, together with fusing an adaptive asymptotic funnel control approach, a funnel-based formation control
strategy is constructed. In comparison to the current outcomes,
this strategy demonstrates the subsequent salient characteristics:
1) enhancing the robustness of the considered system subject to such
practical factors; 2) achieving the asymptotic formation control
with predefined global funnel performance constraints; 3) lowering computational and structural complexity without function
approximation, switching of the controller structure, and complex
nonlinear terms. Ultimately, the theoretical assertions are validated
via the simulation paradigm.
Index Terms—Asymptotic stability, distributed formation
control, funnel control, heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs systems, robust
adaptive control.

due to its broad application potential [1], [2], [3]. Its primary
goal is to stabilize the relative positions of heterogeneous MASs
through local interactions, thereby establishing and maintaining
a predefined geometric formation. As a representative case,
the uncrewed aerial vehicles-uncrewed ground vehicles (UAVsUGVs) system [4], comprising multiple UAVs and UGVs, significantly extends the search radius and service coverage by
leveraging air-ground coordination and complementary functionalities [5]. Consequently, the heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs
system enables tasks that are difficult for single-vehicle formations [6], [7] and is widely applied in 3D mapping [8], hybrid
path planning [9], surveillance-reconnaissance mission [10], etc.
The distributed formation control problem for heterogeneous
UAVs-UGVs systems has been thoroughly explored recently.
Current advancements include a disturbance-resistant UAVsUGVs formation [10] via distributed average tracking, a fault
tolerant adaptive formation control framework [4], a human-inthe-loop 3D coordination [11] through unified models. However,
in [4], [10], [11], the attitude dynamics of each UAV was
ignored. Actually, the UAV must be maneuvered into a specific
attitude to produce the required acceleration to achieve the
target position [12]. Therefore, considering both the position
and attitude dynamics of the UAV is essential. Additionally,
in practical applications, the system parameters of the heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs system are prone to varying during
operation [13] and inter-vehicle communication channels may
suffer from uncertainties [14] such as packet loss [15], random
quantization [16], and measurement noise [17]. These practical
factors pose significant challenges for distributed formation control of heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs systems, particularly when
precision in formation specification is required.
Defining the precision of formation specification often depends on the transient and steady-state values of the relative
position errors between the neighboring vehicles or leader.
To achieve a required precision, funnel control methods [18],
[19], [20], as an effective tool, have been broadly utilized
to ensure the system attains the desired transient and steadystate control performance. Alternatively, the prescribed performance control (PPC) approach [21], [22], [23] serves as another
tool for preassigning both transient and steady-state behaviors.

Inspired by their nature, numerous funnel/PPC-based control
methods were put forward for heterogeneous uncertain MASs.
In [24], the event-triggered synchronization control issue of the
heterogeneous uncertain UAVs-UGVs system with prescribed
performance was studied, while neural networks were utilized in
controller design. Similarly, several funnel/PPC control methods
were constructed in [25], [26], [27] for heterogeneous uncertain
MASs based on fuzzy-approximation approach. However, the
employment of function approximation drastically increases the
complexity of the controller [28], [29], [30].
Different from the function approximation-based funnel/PPC
control methods, the low-complexity funnel/PPC control without using function approximation has been widely investigated. To mention a few, [29] and [30] with relative-degree-two
MASs, [31] with marine surface vessels, and [32], [33] with
mismatched uncertainties MASs. Nevertheless, two primary
concerns still require attention. One concern is that the feasibility of these control laws depends on local, known initial
conditions, which is a restrictive requirement for heterogeneous
UAVs-UGVs systems where platform heterogeneity inherently
leads to distributed, unknown initial states. While recent advances [34], [35], [36] progressively relax these assumptions for
SISO systems, their reliance on homogeneous state dimensions
renders them inapplicable to cross-domain systems where aerial
and ground vehicles exhibit fundamentally different dynamic
orders. The second concern is that merely confining the concerned error within a predefined funnel, which leads to a bounded
steady-state error (referred to as “practical stability”), is insufficient for precision tasks such as cooperative payload transport
that require asymptotic convergence. More recently, non-smooth
feedback approaches [37], [38], [39] achieve asymptotic tracking but at the cost of structural complexity incompatible with
the computational constraints of heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs
systems. To sum up, these two unresolved challenges motivate
our investigation into smooth asymptotic global funnel control
strategy in a low-complexity form for heterogeneous UAVsUGVs systems. Specifically, the integration of such control
solutions onto heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs systems necessitates
controller designs with inherently low computational complexity, while maintaining the cross-domain coordination requirements of global initial-condition independence and asymptotic
precision.
Motivated by the preceding observations, we construct a new
adaptive asymptotic funnel control approach for designing a
robust distributed formation control strategy for heterogeneous
UAVs-UGVs systems. Specifically, the major innovations of this
study are outlined below.
1) A fairly general class of heterogeneous UAVs-UGVs systems with communication noises and dynamics uncertainties is considered, where both the position and attitude
dynamics of UAVs are simultaneously involved.
2) By applying a nonlinear function together with a specific funnel function, a clever transformation is performed
such that the initial error consistently remains within
a non-empty open set. Building upon this transformation, an adaptive asymptotic funnel control approach is
developed by integrating the bound estimation method

with funnel control. This approach guarantees asymptotic
formation control while satisfying arbitrarily predefined
global funnel performance constraints, in contrast to the
current funnel/PPC methods in [29], [30], [31], [32], [33],
which require known initial conditions and merely attain
“practical stability.”
3) Compared with the existing funnel-based asymptotic results [37], [38], [39], the proposed strategy eliminates
the need for controller structure switching by introducing
compensating terms in the form of smooth functions with
respect to formation errors and certain Lebesgue integrable functions. Furthermore, it incorporates fewer nonlinear terms, which is achieved by leveraging the adaptive
law’s inherent properties with suitable scaling.
The remainder is structured as follows. Section II presents
the mathematical preliminaries and problem formulation. Section III shows the main results, containing the proposal of a
robust distributed formation control and its stability analysis.
The theoretical outcomes are demonstrated using numerical
simulations in Section IV, and the entire paper is concluded
in Section V.
