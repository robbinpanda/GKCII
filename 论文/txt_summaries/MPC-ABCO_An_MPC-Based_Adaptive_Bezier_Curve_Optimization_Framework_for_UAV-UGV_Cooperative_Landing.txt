MPC-ABCO: An MPC-Based Adaptive Bezier Curve
Optimization Framework for UAV-UGV
Cooperative Landing
Haiqi Li , Lei Qiang, Zihao Wu , Jiajun Chen , Yinsong Sun , and Xinbo Li

Abstract—This letter addresses the approach of enabling a UAV
to autonomously land on an agile UGV that moves irregularly with
sudden accelerations, decelerations or abrupt turns, where existing
methods have limitations in handling nonlinear dynamics and visual recognition errors in high-dynamic situations. To overcome
these limitations, we present a real-time UAV-UGV cooperative
landing framework that integrates Model Predictive Control and
Adaptive Bezier Curve Optimization (MPC-ABCO) to achieve
UGV’s position prediction and UAV’s trajectory forecasting, ensuring robust performance in high-dynamic scenarios. MPC-ABCO
utilizes an MPC model to predict the UGV’s future states by
leveraging odometry data to model UGV’s kinematic dynamics.
Concurrently, the UAV’s landing trajectory is optimized by Bezier
curves, with waypoints dynamically updated by considering UGV’s
relative future positions detected by AprilTag code. Experimental
results demonstrate that the proposed framework achieves an average landing deviation below 5 cm for UGV’s steering velocities up to
5 m/s under no-acceleration stations. When the UGV follows irregular paths with random accelerations, MPC-ABCO outperforms
traditional strategies in both landing success rate and accuracy,
maintaining reliable performance at maximum velocities of 5 m/s.
Index Terms—Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV), dynamic
landing, computer vision, model predictive control (MPC), Bezier
curve, multi-robot system.

II. CHALLENGES AND PROPOSED MODEL
A. Preliminaries of Dynamic Landing
Dynamic landing of UAVs on UGVs faces two main limitations: nonlinear motion dynamics of UGVs and limitations of
sensor-based localization, which together disrupt reliable state
estimation, trajectory planning, and closed-loop control.
Current approaches either focus on static or low-dynamic
scenarios [4], [10], [21], [22]. Traditional trajectory generators
fail to anticipate UGV motion, leading to lag behind UGV future
states, thus causing landing failure in high-dynamic scenarios.
Closed-loop control systems often use simplistic error correction, unable to handle the nonlinearity and uncertainty inherent
in agile UGV motions. The convergence of UGV nonlinear
dynamics and sensor limitations has created a complex control
problem where accurate state prediction, adaptive trajectory generation, and robust feedback control must be tightly integrated.
To overcome these limitations, we propose a unified framework that models UGV motion with sufficient fidelity, fuses
heterogeneous sensor data (UGV odometry and UAV vision)
in a closed-loop manner, and generates trajectories that respect
both the UAV dynamics and real-time constraints.

B. Proposed Framework
MPC-ABCO has three modules: real-time UGV state estimation, UAV trajectory generation and PID controller. Notably,
the UGV continuously transmits its odometry data to the UAV
through wireless communication, forming the foundational input for cooperative control.The closed-loop cooperation of these
modules enables the landing process to be smooth and precise
in high-dynamic scenarios.
The MPC-based prediction module firstly provides predicted
position information for UAV trajectory generation module.
Running on the UAV’s onboard computer, this module leverages
the received UGV odometry data to predict position deviations via MPC algorithm, quantifying the potential mismatch
between the UGV’s actual motion and its predicted trajectory.
The generated trajectory points are then sent to the PID controller. As the UAV flies and the UGV moves, the prediction
module continuously updates the UGV’s predicted states by
rolling optimization, and the trajectory module acquires the
updated predicted position combined with the UAV’s visually
detected AprilTag position as the end point to regenerate the
trajectory. This closed-loop operation is able to handle dynamic
environmental changes and vehicle uncertainties effectively.
To elucidate, when the UGV moves randomly, MPC-ABCO
could compensate for sudden influences and handle extreme
interference even if computer vision is transiently unable to
detect continuously. The corresponding framework is shown in
Fig. 2.
