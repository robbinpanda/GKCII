Abstract—This paper investigates a path planning approach
for cooperative unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) and unmanned
ground vehicle (UGV) systems. Considering the position error
corrections (PEC) of UAV, threats, and other complex constraints,
a UAV-UGV cooperative path planning model is developed to
minimize the total cost. Then, a Hybrid Ant-Pigeon Cooperative Optimization (HAPCO) algorithm is presented to address
issues in existing bio-inspired optimization algorithms. A new
pheromone update strategy and adaptive inertia weight are
designed to optimise the performance. the performance of the
Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) algorithm and Pigeon-inspired
Optimization (PIO) algorithm. Moreover, the two algorithms
are integrated to perform a full-cycle search. Simulation results reveal that the HAPCO algorithm outperforms ACO and
PIO regarding path quality, cooperation cost, convergence, and
computational efficiency, further demonstrated the validity and
superiority of our algorithm.
Index Terms—path planning, unmanned autonomous systems,
bio-inspired optimization, obstacle avoidance

I. I NTRODUCTION
Artificial intelligence-enhanced unmanned autonomous systems are capable of performing tasks in complex environments.
They are employed extensively in military, industrial, and
civilian contexts [1]–[4]. In particular, the capacity of UAV
and UGV to undertake tasks collaboratively has introduced
novel tactical advantages to contemporary military operations.
Effective collaboration between UAV and UGV not only
enhances the efficiency of task execution but also improves
operational flexibility and safety.
Path planning, as a key technology for the cooperative
task execution of UAVs and UGVs, has recently emerged
as a prominent area of research [5], [6]. Ropero et al. [7]
proposed a path planning method that combines the Voronoi
diagram and gravitational optimization for UAV-UGV cooperative exploration tasks. Zhang et al. designed a series
of improved A-Star algorithms to achieve proactive threat
avoidance for aircraft [8]–[10]. However, these methods are
characterised by high computational complexity and limited
flexibility when dealing with high-dimensional optimization

In the new pheromone update strategy, the pheromone
concentration on the current optimal path can be dynamically
adjusted based on the conditions of the paths searched by
the ants. When a better path exists, a significant difference
between Lk and Lbest , which indicates that the proportion of
ants traversing the current optimal path is tiny. Therefore, the
intensity of the pheromone on the current optimal path can be
dynamically increased to attract more ants by using Eq. (12).
As the iterations progress, the pheromone concentration on
the current optimal path will continuously increase, causing
more and more ants to tend to cluster along this path. At
this point, the pheromone concentration will subsequently
decrease. When the pheromone concentration reaches zero, the
pheromone evaporation occurring solely on the current optimal
path will weaken the pheromone, effectively preventing the
ACO algorithm from getting trapped in local optima.
A negative pheromone concentration can reduce the accumulated pheromone concentration on the path and shorten the
time required to search for the optimal path. This dynamic
pheromone updating strategy allows the pheromone concentration to play a full role on the current optimal path, while
preventing the algorithm from stagnating due to an excessive
increase in its concentration.

but cannot find acceptable local searches under complex constraints [19]. To overcome the limitations, a HAPCO algorithm
is developed to tackle global and local optimization challenges.
